Florence Nightingale

Florence Nightingale was an English social reformer and the pioneer of modern nursing. She was born in Florence, Italy on May 12, 1820. She was known for reforming the professional nursing services in the Crimean War, establishing her nickname as "Lady with the Lamp". Later on, she built St. Thomas' Hospital and the Nightingale Training School for Nurses. Her work has helped improved the British healthcare system. She passed away on August 13, 1910 in London.
